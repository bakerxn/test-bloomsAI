About & Guide

Why we built this tool, how to use it, and the frameworks that inform it.

Why this tool

Educators are navigating rapid changes in AI, and we want a simple, ethical, and research-grounded way to design activities that foster critical thinking. This site helps you combine the Revised Bloom’s Taxonomy (Iowa State / Rex Heer) with an AI literacy focus (Dr. Kara Kennedy) to generate ideas and prompts for your teaching context.

How to use

  1. Choose one option from each category: Cognitive Process, Knowledge Dimension, and AI Literacy Focus.
  2. Click Continue to see examples, assessment ideas, and accessibility considerations.
  3. Open the Customization Form and enter your course context. We’ll generate a long-form prompt.
  4. Pick your AI tool. We copy your prompt to clipboard and open the tool in a new tab so you can iterate.

Frameworks

  • Revised Bloom’s Taxonomy (Cognitive Process & Knowledge Dimensions): A Model of Learning Objectives by Rex Heer, Iowa State University (CC BY-SA 4.0).
  • AI Literacy Framework by Dr. Kara Kennedy, mapped to UNESCO Digital Literacy Global Framework (2023) (CC BY 4.0).

AI use statement

We encourage transparent, intentional, and accessible uses of generative AI. When using AI tools, disclose the tool and version when relevant; verify claims and citations; protect privacy; follow institutional policies; respect intellectual property; and consider environmental impact. The prompts we generate are starting points—human judgment and disciplinary expertise remain central.
